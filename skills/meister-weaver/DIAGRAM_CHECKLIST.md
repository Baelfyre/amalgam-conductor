# Diagram Checklist

## Diagram Objective
- [ ] Diagram answers one clear question and states current, proposed, or illustrative status.

## Audience
- [ ] Vocabulary, notation, and abstraction level fit the intended reader.

## Boundary
- [ ] System, process, trust, or deployment boundary is explicit when relevant.

## Actors
- [ ] External roles are evidence-backed and remain outside the system boundary.

## Classes
- [ ] Classes, responsibilities, visibility, and OOP relationships match code or approved design.

## Tables
- [ ] Entities, keys, and attributes match schema evidence and the chosen ERD level.

## Components
- [ ] Components have clear responsibilities, ownership, and interfaces.

## Relationships
- [ ] Direction and relationship type are correct and supported.

## Cardinality
- [ ] Multiplicity and optionality are shown only when known.

## Sequence Order
- [ ] Initiating actor, messages, returns, alternatives, failures, and outcome follow verified order.

## Layers
- [ ] UI, service, repository, database, and deployment concerns are not conflated.

## External Systems
- [ ] External dependencies, protocols, data direction, and ownership are labeled when known.

## Naming
- [ ] Names match source artifacts and use one convention.

## Legend
- [ ] Nonstandard colors, lines, icons, and labels are explained.

## Readability
- [ ] Text is readable, crossings are limited, and unrelated objectives are split.

## Missing Evidence
- [ ] Unsupported elements are omitted or marked as assumptions.
- [ ] Missing facts needed for correctness are listed.

## Validation
- [ ] Mermaid or PlantUML parses or renders when possible.
- [ ] Original artifact is preserved unless overwrite was requested.
