# Privacy Checklist

## Data Classification
- [ ] Data categories, sensitivity, owners, locations, and permitted handling are documented.

## Personal Data Identification
- [ ] Direct and indirect identifiers are identified across collection, storage, logs, exports, and integrations.

## Sensitive Data Identification
- [ ] Credentials, financial, health, biometric, location, protected-category, and other high-impact data are identified when relevant.

## Data Minimization
- [ ] Each collected field is necessary for a documented objective.

## Purpose Limitation
- [ ] Collection, use, sharing, and secondary use remain within documented purposes.

## Retention
- [ ] Retention, deletion, archive, backup expiry, and exception ownership are defined.

## Access Control
- [ ] Personal data access is least-privilege, reviewable, and revocable.

## Consent or Lawful Basis Documentation
- [ ] Relevant governance documentation exists and is reviewed by qualified stakeholders when required.
- [ ] The technical review does not make legal conclusions.

## Data Subject Rights Awareness
- [ ] Access, correction, deletion, restriction, objection, or portability workflows are documented when applicable.

## Logging of Sensitive Data
- [ ] Logs avoid secrets and unnecessary personal data and have appropriate access and retention.

## Privacy Documentation
- [ ] Data inventory, flows, purposes, recipients, processors, retention, and safeguards are current.

## Privacy Risk Evidence
- [ ] Risks identify affected people, impact, likelihood, controls, owners, and residual risk.

## Missing Privacy Evidence
- [ ] Unknown data flows, third parties, retention rules, backups, exports, and user-rights processes are listed explicitly.
